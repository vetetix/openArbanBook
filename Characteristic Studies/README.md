**README**

Most of the openArbanBook has been transcribed from the earliest English language version that is commonly available.
Although this is also an edited version, it was decided in the beginning of this project that it would be the most authentic starting point. However, it appears that the versions of the Characteristic Studies that are published in this version of the book have big sections missing compared to the modern editions and has a lot of inconsistencies in the articulations.

Ultimately it doesn't really make sense to release copies of the studies that are not as people would wish to play them in modern times but for now the work will be done just for the sake of keeping things moving forward and preserving the earliest edition. Eventually these versions will then be packaged up as the "1879 Characteristic Studies" or similar.

The next decision to be made is which version to use. We need to be sure not to be violating copyright of current editions of the book - that's the entire purpose of this project! It may, in fact, be better to release the most generic versions of these studies as possible to encourage students and performers to interpret the work for themselves.

Remember that the primary goal of oAP is to create files that people can freely copy, edit and share - this is the best way to make sure that sheet music that should be free of charge is easily available and a better option than pirating a copyrighted version of the book. These currently copyrighted versions have no right or valuable merit to justify the charging of money for the historical knowledge pertaining to our art.
